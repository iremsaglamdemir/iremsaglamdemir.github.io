Labor Market Frictions and the Transition to a Green Economy

This paper investigates how introducing labor market frictions and endogenous TFP growth can affect the effectiveness of economic policies in the transition to a green economy. Since producing with lower emissions requires a higher level of technology and training, this imposes mobility costs on the labor force in moving to a green sector. Considering this substantial cost, policies that only aim to provide capital services for the green sector (like green quantitative easing) might provide a lower speed of transition than the CO2 tax policy. This study analyzes the transitional dynamics to a greener economy and compares the speed of transition under fiscal and monetary policies.
